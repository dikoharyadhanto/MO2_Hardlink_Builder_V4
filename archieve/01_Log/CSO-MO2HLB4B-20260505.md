## 4. Architectural & Strategic Decision Trace

* **Decisions Made**:
  - Incremental must be **mod-driven**, not file-driven.
  - Use **manifest snapshot comparison (size + mtime)** as change detector.
  - Avoid hash except in exceptional scenarios.
  - Introduce **impact-based ownership re-resolution**.
  - Use **relink-over-verify** strategy for hardlink updates.

* **Rationale (The "Why")**:
  - File-level scanning is too expensive at scale.
  - Hardlink operations are cheaper than verification.
  - Ownership constraints require selective graph updates, not full recomputation.

* **Acknowledged Trade-offs**:
  - Accept false positives (unnecessary relink) to avoid false negatives (missed changes).
  - Sacrifice perfect verification accuracy for performance.
  - Accept added complexity in ownership resolution logic.

* **Rejected Alternatives**:
  - *Full hash-based verification* → **Rejected Because**: Too slow, defeats incremental purpose  
  - *Always full rebuild* → **Rejected Because**: No scalability benefit  
  - *Target-based comparison (source vs target)* → **Rejected Because**: Inefficient and redundant
